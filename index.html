Audio samples from "StyleTTS: Stylized Text-to-Speech with Fine-Grained Style Disentanglement, Control and Transfer"

Authors: Ruimin Wang, Fanbo Meng, Kai Liu, Wei Chen

Abstract:
Speech synthesis is used in many scenarios, while different scenarios require synthesized speech with different styles. Generally, the style of synthesized speech is closely related to the recording style of the speaker. In this work, we propose StyleTTS, a model that can make a speaker synthesize different styles by learning from other speakers. This model supports the disentanglement of prosody and timbre to attain the goal of cross speaker style control. The model utilizes frame-level VAE to extract fine-grained prosody information from speech, then uses two different modules to model timbre and prosody. During inference time, we combine timbre and prosody from different speakers to achieve style transfer. Experiments show that our model has good performance in style control, like multiple scenes, dialect accent control synthesis, and also on crosslanguage synthesis.

Methods meaning
GT: Ground-truth speech of target style.
StyleTTS-target: Upper bound method using the target style speech training both decoder and prosody predictor.
StyleTTS-source : Baseline method using the source timbre speech training both decoder and prosody predictor.
StyleTTS-p&e : Baseline method using the source timbre speech training decoder and using the target style speech training prosody predictor, while the prosody extractor uses normalized pitch and energy.
StyleTTS-transfer : Our method using the source timbre speech training decoder and using the target style speech training prosody predictor, while the prosody extractor uses frame-level VAE.
